Introduction

With the invention of the “cloud” and the SaaS (Software as a Service), it is possible to access our saved data with any computer connected to the internet in the world (it represent 4,12 billions of people). So, if you don’t want your data to fall into anyone’s hands, you have to protect it with a password, or even more, you could encrypt the whole message. At the start, the servers simply stocked your password, then came the encryption codes like Vigenere’s and finally, the hash function, nearly inviolable.
So, we can ask us if people can gain access to our data, how can they do, and how to effectively protect it. We can also wonder to what point encryption can secure our data ?
In this website, we will give you some informations about Encryption.
In the History part, we will tell you the evolution of encryption methods. In the Method part we will talk about some methods of encryption. And in the Interception part, we will explain how our data is secured, if the protection is powerful enough, and why there is a rise in the number of hackings and what solutions we have about that problem.


Definition

Encryption consists in transforming a message with an algorithm or a logical system, which will make it incomprehensible. Those messages will be secured, and unauthorized people (people who don’t know how to decrypt the messages) will not be able to understand it, even if they somehow managed to find the coded message. It uses an algorithm which modifies the original letters of the text or, harder, hashes the message (we will get more into the detail later on) to ensure the security of the data. Encryption exclusively used for texts, but can also be used to encrypt audio messages, images, banking transactions and even more. But, we will focus only on encrypted texts on this website.
